CRIME SCENE AND EVIDENCE PHOTOGRAPHY

Crime Scene Photography:

  • Document the overall scene
  • Capture details of evidence and injuries
  • Record scene dimensions and layout
  • Photograph from multiple angles and distances
  • Use scales and measuring devices for reference
  • Document lighting conditions
  • Capture images in RAW format
  • Use a tripod for stability

Evidence Photography:

  • Close-up shots of evidence
  • Document evidence tags and labels
  • Capture images of evidence in context
  • Use macro photography for small evidence
  • Photograph evidence from multiple angles
  • Use scales and measuring devices for reference
  • Document evidence packaging and storage
  • Capture images of evidence in various lighting conditions

Best Practices:

  • Use high-quality cameras and lenses
  • Ensure proper lighting and exposure
  • Avoid distortion and perspective errors
  • Use a consistent naming convention
  • Document camera settings and metadata
  • Store images securely and backup data
  • Follow agency protocols and guidelines
  • Continuously update skills and knowledge

Types of Evidence Photography:

  • Forensic photography
  • Crime scene photography
  • Evidence documentation
  • Injury photography
  • Surveillance photography
  • Accident reconstruction photography
  • Fire and arson photography
  • Digital evidence photography

Equipment:

  • DSLR or mirrorless cameras
  • Lenses (wide-angle, macro, telephoto)
  • Tripods and stabilizers
  • Lighting equipment (flash, LED)
  • Scales and measuring devices
  • Evidence markers and labels
  • Camera bags and cases
  • Memory cards and storage devices

Software:

  • Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop
  • Evidence management software
  • Image editing and enhancement tools
  • Measurement and analysis software
  • 3D modeling and reconstruction software

Remember, high-quality crime scene and evidence photography is crucial for investigations, court proceedings, and justice.
