Key Steps in a Solved Detective Investigation:
Initial Response:
Crime Scene Securing: The crime scene is secured to prevent contamination of evidence.
Preliminary Assessment: Initial observations are made, and crucial evidence is identified and preserved.
Evidence Collection and Preservation:
Physical Evidence: Collection of fingerprints, DNA, weapons, and other tangible items.
Documentation: Detailed photographs, sketches, and notes of the crime scene and evidence.
Chain of Custody: Ensuring that evidence is properly logged and tracked to maintain its integrity.

Forensic Analysis:
Lab Testing: Sending collected evidence to forensic laboratories for analysis (e.g., DNA testing, ballistic analysis).
Expert Analysis: Utilizing experts to interpret forensic evidence and provide insights.
Investigation and Follow-up:
Lead Tracking: Following up on leads and tips received from the public or other sources.
Surveillance: Monitoring suspects and locations to gather additional evidence.
Collaboration and Information Sharing:
Inter-agency Cooperation: Working with other law enforcement agencies, forensic experts, and legal authorities.
Public Appeals: Engaging the public through media for information and assistance.
Arrest and Legal Proceedings:
Arrest: Apprehending the suspect based on accumulated evidence.
Charging: Formally charging the suspect with the crime.
Trial: Presenting evidence in court to secure a conviction.
Resolution and Case Closure:
Verdict and Sentencing: Achieving a guilty verdict and appropriate sentencing.
Documentation: Finalizing reports and documenting the resolution of the case
