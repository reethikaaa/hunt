EVIDENCE COLLECTION

Types of Evidence:

  • Physical Evidence (e.g., DNA, fingerprints, weapons)
  • Documentary Evidence (e.g., records, documents, departments)
  • Testimonial Evidence (e.g., witness statements, confessions)
  • Digital Evidence (e.g., computer files, phone data, social media)

Evidence Collection Principles:

  • Preserve and protect the evidence
  • Handle evidence minimally and carefully
  • Document evidence thoroughly
  • Maintain chain of custody
  • Ensure evidence integrity

Evidence Collection Techniques:

  • Photography and videography
  • Measurement and mapping
  • Swabbing and sampling
  • Lifting fingerprints and footwear impressions
  • Collecting biological fluids and tissues

Evidence Handling and Storage:

  • Use appropriate containers and packaging
  • Label and date evidence
  • Store evidence in a secure, climate-controlled environment
  • Limit access to authorized personnel
  • Maintain evidence logs and chain of custody records

Digital Evidence Collection:

  • Imaging and cloning devices
  • Data extraction and recovery
  • Network and department analysis
  • Mobile device forensic analysis
  • Cloud storage and social media analysis

Biological Evidence Collection:

  • DNA sampling and analysis
  • Bloodstain pattern analysis
  • Hair and fiber analysis
  • Forensic anthropology and entomology

Chain of Custody:

  • Document evidence transfer and handling
  • Ensure evidence integrity and security
  • Maintain continuous custody records
  • Verify evidence authenticity

Evidence Submission and Analysis:

  • Submit evidence to appropriate labs and experts
  • Request analysis and testing
  • Review and interpret results
  • Use evidence to support investigations and prosecutions

Remember, proper evidence collection and handling are crucial for ensuring the integrity and admissibility of evidence in criminal investigations and legal proceedings.
