CASE STUDIES

KIDNAPPING

Kidnapping is the act of taking someone away against their will, often using force or deception. Kidnapping can be motivated by ransom demands, personal vendettas, or human trafficking. Victims of kidnapping often experience physical and emotional trauma.

CRIME

Murder is the intentional and unlawful killing of another person, often driven by motives like greed, revenge, or passion. Crimes involving murder can include manslaughter, homicide, and infanticide. Murder has severe legal consequences, including life imprisonment or the death penalty.

HUMAN TRAFFICKING

Human trafficking involves exploiting people for labor, sex, or organs, often using coercion or deception. Traffickers target vulnerable individuals, including children, migrants, and those in poverty. Human trafficking is a global issue, with millions of people trapped in modern-day slavery.

THEFT

Theft involves taking someone else's property without permission, often with the intent to permanently deprive them of it. Types of theft include burglary, robbery, larceny, and shoplifting.
