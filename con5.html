COLLEGE AND UNIVERSITY PROGRAMS

Undergraduate Programs:

  • Bachelor of Science in Forensic Science
  • Bachelor of Science in Criminal Justice with a focus on Forensic Science
  • Bachelor of Science in Biology or Chemistry with a minor in Forensic Science

Graduate Programs:

  • Master of Science in Forensic Science
  • Master of Science in Criminal Justice with a focus on Forensic Science
  • Master of Science in DNA Analysis or Genetic Engineering

Certificate Programs:

  • Certificate in Forensic Science
  • Certificate in Crime Scene Investigation
  • Certificate in Digital Forensics

Online Programs:

  • Online Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Forensic Science
  • Online certificate programs in specialized areas like DNA analysis

Key Courses:

  • Forensic Biology
  • Forensic Chemistry
  • Crime Scene Investigation
  • DNA Analysis
  • Fingerprint Analysis
  • Ballistics
  • Toxicology
  • Forensic Anthropology
  • Criminal Law and Procedure

Lab Facilities and Equipment:

  • State-of-the-art forensic labs
  • DNA sequencing equipment
  • Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS)
  • Scanning electron microscopy (SEM)
  • Crime scene simulation labs

Internship and Job Placement:

  • Internships with law enforcement agencies or forensic labs
  • Job placement assistance
  • Networking opportunities with professionals in the field

Accreditation:

  • Look for programs accredited by the American Academy of Forensic Sciences (AAFS)
  • Accreditation ensures program quality and prepares students for certification exams

Certification Exams:

  • Certified Forensic Scientist (CFS)
  • Certified Crime Scene Investigator (CCSI)
  • Certified DNA Analyst (CDNA)

When choosing a program, consider factors like curriculum, faculty expertise, lab facilities, internship opportunities, and accreditation.
