DETECTIVE COLD CASE

A detective cold case refers to an unsolved criminal investigation that remains open and unresolved for a long time but is eventually solved through renewed efforts, advanced forensic techniques, or new evidence. Solving a cold case typically involves re-examining the original investigation, following up on new leads, and leveraging advancements in technology. Here is a detailed description of a solved detective cold case:
Key Elements of Solving a Cold Case:
Reopening the Case:
Case ReOVER OVER VIEW : Detectives revisit the case files, including all evidence, witness statements, and reports from the original investigation.
Fresh Perspective: New detectives may bring fresh insights and identify overlooked details.
Advanced Forensic Techniques:
DNA Analysis: Utilizing modern DNA testing on old evidence, such as hair, blood, or other biological materials.
Digital Forensics: Examining digital evidence, such as old computer files, departments, or phone records, with contemporary tools.
New Witnesses and Information:
Re-interOVER OVER VIEW ing Witnesses: Mobileing original witnesses who may recall additional details or whose circumstances might have changed.
Public Appeals: Using media to appeal to the public for new information or witnesses.
Technological and Methodological Advances:
Databases: Using modern databases (e.g., CODIS for DNA, AFIS for fingerprints) to match evidence with known offenders.
Geolocation: Employing geolocation data from cell phones or other devices to place suspects at the crime scene.
Collaboration and Persistence:
Task Forces: Forming special cold case units or task forces dedicated to solving old cases.
Collaboration: Working with other law enforcement agencies, forensic experts, and legal entities.
Legal and Judicial Processes:
Re-arrest and Re-trial: If new evidence implicates a suspect, re-arrest and re-trial may follow.
Conviction: Achieving a conviction based on the new evidence and testimonies.

MY SOLVED CASES:


The Golden State Killer Case:
Original Case: Between 1974 and 1986, a series of rapes and murders in California remained unsolved. The perpetrator, known as the Golden State Killer, eluded capture for decades.
Cold Case Status: Despite extensive investigation, the case went cold with no leads.
Advanced Forensics: In 2018, detectives used advanced DNA testing and genetic genealogy to identify relatives of the suspect through public genealogy databases.
Suspect Identification: This process led to Joseph James DeAngelo, a former police officer.
Arrest and Charges: DeAngelo was arrested and charged with multiple counts of murder and sexual assault.
Legal Proceedings: He eventually pleaded guilty to 13 murders and numerous other crimes.
Case Closure: The case was officially closed with his conviction and sentencing to life imprisonment without the possibility of parole.
